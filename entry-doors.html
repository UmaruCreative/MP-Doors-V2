Built for Stability, Not Just Appearance

Material science applied to the entryway.

Fiberglass Panel with Wood Grain

Compression-molded fiberglass reproduces real wood grain depth. Stainable or paintable like wood — but won’t rot, split, or delaminate. Designed to maintain appearance through years of sun and moisture exposure.

Complete Composite Frame System

The entire frame — jambs, sill, header, and brickmold — is composite, not wood. Composite is non-porous and dimensionally stable, maintaining alignment and seal integrity year-round.

Polyurethane Foam Core

R-value approximately 5.8 vs. solid wood near 1.0 — about six times higher thermal resistance. Stabilizes indoor temperature and lowers HVAC demand. Certified by ENERGY STAR.

Multi-Point Locking System

Locks engage the frame at three or more points — top, middle, and bottom. Composite frame rigidity supports this system without splitting or loosening over time.

Factory-Assembled & Prefinished

Each unit arrives fully assembled: panel hung in frame, hinges aligned, seals installed, and finish applied. Standard replacements typically install in 3–4 hours.

Common Problems Solved — And Why They Happen

Seasonal sticking or gaps

Wood expands with humidity and shrinks when dry. Composite expansion is minimal, so alignment stays consistent across seasons.

Frequent repainting or fading

Wood requires refinishing every few years due to UV and moisture exposure. Fiberglass is UV-stable and non-porous. Maintenance is typically limited to occasional cleaning.

Weak or deteriorating frame

Moisture softens wood and creates stress fractures near locks. Composite maintains structural strength and resists splitting under repeated locking pressure.

Wanting real wood aesthetics

Deep grain embossing and stainable finishes provide authentic appearance without rot, cracking, or refinishing cycles.
