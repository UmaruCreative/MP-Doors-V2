Why Standard Doors Fail

The Hidden Leak

"Does water seep under your patio door after heavy rain? That small, intermittent leak is usually the first sign of a much larger problem: trapped moisture in the bottom rail."

Last spring we removed a sill on a coastal job; the timber behind it had rotted while the visible trim looked fine. In one coastal replacement we measured 12 mm of bottom-rail distortion after about 4 years of exposure — enough to break seals and raise winter heat loss by roughly 18%.

Moisture trapped in the sill does the damage quietly. A well-designed composite sill diverts and drains water before it ever reaches the rail — and that small detail is what makes the difference between a door that needs replacing in a few years and one that lasts decades.

MP Doors builds that sill into every unit.

Engineered for Long-Term Performance

Built around water management construction to address the root causes of door failure.

100% Waterproof Composite Frame

Non-porous composite resists moisture absorption that causes timber rot and vinyl distortion. The bottom rail—the usual failure point—keeps its shape through repeated wet–dry cycles.

Hydroshield Protection System

Small but decisive: integrated drainage channels, a composite sill that won’t soak up water, multi-layer weatherstripping and sealed construction. These features stop water from lodging in the frame.

Smooth-Glide Stainless Rollers

Sealed ball-bearing rollers support 200+ lb insulated glass panels and are rated for 50,000+ cycles. Adjustable for long-term alignment; cheaper plastic rollers often wear out quickly.

Low-E Glass with Argon Fill

Dual-pane Low-E glazing with argon reduces heat transfer by roughly 40% compared with standard single glazing — a tangible saving on large patio openings.

Factory-Assembled System

Each door is assembled and tested in a controlled factory environment. Typical replacement install: 4–6 hours for pre-hung vs 8–12 hours for field-built systems.

Problems This Design Prevents

Door becomes harder to slide

Not just dirt — frame distortion from trapped moisture or thermal movement causes friction. A drained sill and stable composite frame keep operation smooth.

Water pooling or leaks

Porous frames and missing drainage let water in. The Hydroshield sill detail diverts driven rain and pooled water before it reaches the structure.

Spiking energy bills

Air infiltration and poor glass performance cause loss. Tight composite framing plus Low-E argon glazing reduces transmission and leakage.

Repeated replacements

If your previous door failed from a moist sill or saturated rail, replacing like for like will repeat the problem. MP Doors addresses the root cause.
